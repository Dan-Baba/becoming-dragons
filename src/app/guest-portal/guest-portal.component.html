THINGS TO DO IN AND AROUND TOWN, PLUS MORE

There is so much to do where we live and in the surrounding areas. Our wedding will most likely be held in Schuylerville, NY, and to our home in Victory Mills, NY. It’s a 15-minute drive into Saratoga Springs, NY, and 45 minutes to Albany, NY, our state’s capital. It is also at the base of the Adirondack mountains, 2.5 hours from Burrlington, VT, 3.5 hours from NYC, 3.5 hours from Boston, MA, 4 hours from Montreal, Canada, 4.5 hours from Philadelphia, PA, and 5 hours from Niagara Falls, NY. Here are some of our favorite day trips:

Saratoga Springs, NY
Play tourist in the city Gwen grew up in; whether you are from here or not, there is always something new to find in Saratoga. Fun fact: Potato chips were invented in Saratoga Springs, NY!

Saratoga Performing Arts Center (or SPAC) in Saratoga Spa State Park
108 Avenue of the Pines, Saratoga Springs, NY 12866 Located inside a beautiful New York State Park, this indoor/outdoor concert venue is a great spot to visit while you’re in town. Find a concert or just walk around!

Roosevelt Baths & Spa in Saratoga Spa State Park
39 Roosevelt Dr, Saratoga Springs, NY 12866 Also located inside the park but deserves its own callout. If you are a Saratoga Springs resident, you get a discount on baths and spa services. If you are from out of town, at least check out the mineral baths. Saratoga Springs is one of only a few places in the world with naturally occurring carbonated mineral spring water. You can also taste the spring water by visiting the different springs around the park and in Saratoga’s downtown Congress Park.

Saratoga’s Downtown
Congress Park, 1 E Congress St, Saratoga Springs, NY 12866
I would start and end with Congress Park (across the street is the Visitor’s Center). Downtown is home to more naturally occurring springs, the Canfield Casino (take a historic tour), the carousel, awesome local shops and restaurants, as well as where Gwen and Dan both work.

Outside of Saratoga
Schuylerville, NY/Victory Mills, NY, is a beautiful and historic area. If you’re into taking long walks and/or history, this is the place for you. General Schuyler’s house, Saratoga Monument and Victory Woods are right down the road from Gwen and Dan’s home—practically in their backyard.

Saratoga National Historical Park (Saratoga Battlefield)
648 NY-32, Stillwater, NY 12170
Here, in 1777, during the American War for Independence, American troops battled and beat a British invasion force, marking the first time in world history that a British Army ever surrendered. These are beautiful grounds for walking, running, biking and learning some American history along the way.

Ithaca, NY
Gwen’s home away from home. Ithaca is known for its nature, beautiful natural gorges, waterfalls (Taughannock Falls State Park is Gwen’s favorite), hikes, state parks, the Finger Lakes and wine. “Ithaca is gorges” is not only a perfect tagline but is also so true.

Massachusetts Museum of Contemporary Art (Mass MoCA)
1040 Mass MoCA Way, North Adams, MA 01247
Contemporary art museum in a quaint little town in Massachusetts. Gwen likes to make the trip at least once a year, usually around her birthday.
